Organic Fertilizers

Compost

Uses:

  • Enhances soil fertility
  • Improves soil structure
  • Increases water retention capacity
  • Suppresses plant diseases and pests
  • Reduces the need for chemical fertilizers

Advantages:

  • Sustainable and eco-friendly
  • Low-cost and easy to produce
  • Improves soil health over time
  • Reduces soil erosion
  • Enhances plant growth and yield

Availability:

  • Widely available, can be produced at home or purchased commercially
  • Ingredients are readily available, such as kitchen scraps and yard waste

Awareness:

  • Increasing awareness, especially among organic farmers and gardeners
  • Government initiatives and educational programs promote composting

Vermicompost

Uses:

  • Excellent organic fertilizer rich in nutrients
  • Improves soil aeration and water retention
  • Suppresses soil-borne diseases and pests
  • Enhances plant growth and yield
  • Reduces chemical fertilizer use

Advantages:

  • Highly nutrient-rich
  • Odor-free and easy to handle
  • Fast decomposition process
  • Can be used on a variety of plants
  • Reduces waste and promotes sustainability

Availability:

  • Commercially available in various forms
  • Can be produced at home using vermicomposting bins

Awareness:

  • Growing awareness among organic farmers and gardeners
  • Government and NGOs promote vermicomposting for sustainable agriculture

Green Manure

Uses:

  • Enhances soil fertility by adding organic matter
  • Improves soil structure
  • Suppresses weeds
  • Protects soil from erosion
  • Increases water-holding capacity

Advantages:

  • Sustainable and eco-friendly
  • Low-cost and easy to implement
  • Improves soil health over time
  • Reduces the need for chemical fertilizers
  • Can be integrated into crop rotation

Availability:

  • Depends on local climate and soil conditions

Awareness:

  • Increasing awareness among farmers, especially those practicing organic farming
  • Government extension services promote green manure practices

Fish Emulsion

Uses:

  • Boosts plant immunity

Advantages:

  • Quickly absorbed by plants
  • Highly concentrated nutrient source
  • Can be used in small quantities
  • Environmentally friendly
  • Improves soil microbial activity

Availability:

  • Commercially available as liquid or powder form
  • Can be produced at home by fermenting fish waste

Awareness:

  • Increasing awareness among organic farmers and gardeners
  • Used in both conventional and organic agriculture
